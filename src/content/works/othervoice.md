A prosthetic voice instrument — a wearable device that extends the performer's voice through transducers, speakers, and real-time processing. The instrument creates a second voice that shadows and transforms the first, blurring the boundary between body and machine, speech and music.

Worn on the body, the device picks up vocal vibrations through contact with the throat and chest, processes them through analog and digital chains, and projects the result through small speakers and transducers mounted on the performer.

The instrument is ongoing — each iteration explores different relationships between the biological voice and its electronic double.
