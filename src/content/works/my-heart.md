A flickering, phantasmagoric exploration of memory, composed for ten screens and eight speakers in the [RML CineChamber](http://rml-cinechamber.org/) at [Gray Area](https://grayarea.org/) in San Francisco. Photos of New York and Los Angeles were processed into point clouds, ghostly digital models that omitted moving objects, leaving the cities empty and scattered with unidentifiable digital artifacts. Streaks of sky and brick walls become similar consistencies while signs disintegrate into formless color as you move past them.

The sound, drawn from ambisonic wind and ocean recordings and granular synthesis, fractures spatial cues until the listener is in many places at once. Rising motifs, reminiscent of smoke, dissipate as they rise while voices condense as they descend. Eventually, we return to the waves, the listener's position static for the first time as the ocean ebbs around them.
